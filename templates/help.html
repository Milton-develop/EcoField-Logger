Fieldwork Instructions & User Guide

1. Using the 5‑Step Logger

  • Step 1 – Researcher Information
    • Enter your Year Group (e.g. 2025/26).
    • Enter your correct Group ID exactly as provided by your lecturer.
    • Enter your Name as it appears on the class register.
    • Enter your STUDENT ID as issued to you by the University
  • Step 2 – Location, GPS & Photos
    • Provide a short Location Description (e.g. “Near large river bank, south transect”).
    • Select the Survey Type (Water, Habitat, or Biodiversity) that best matches your activity.
    • Click 📍 Get Current Coordinates to autofill Latitude and Longitude.
    • Allow your browser to use Location Services and make sure you are on https://.
    • Upload or take Field Photos of the site and/or species. Thumbnails will appear in the preview grid.
  • Step 3 – Species & Survey Type
    • Enter the Location name.
    • Select the Species name from the data provided
    • Record the Number of individuals observed.
    • In the case of a New Species tick the checkbox to provide a new section for your new entry and record
    • Select the primary Habitat (Grassland, Forest, Rocky, Other).
  • Step 4 – Microhabitat: Physical Conditions
    • Temperature (°C)
    • Humidity (%)
    • Rainfall (mm) – if measured during your survey period.
    • Wind Speed (m/s)
    • Wind Direction (°) – in degrees from North if available.
  • Step 5 – Microhabitat: Light & Vegetation
    • Light Intensity (lx)
    • Canopy Cover (%) – estimated percentage of sky covered by vegetation.
    • Canopy Height (m)
    • Use the Notes box for extra observations (e.g. behaviour, disturbances, unusual weather, sampling method).
  • Step Navigation
    • Use the numbered circles 1–5 at the top of the form or the Next/Previous buttons to move between steps.
    • Review all entries before clicking Submit Observation on Step 5.

2. Measurement Units Reference

To keep the dataset consistent across all groups, these units will be used for every measurement:

Variable Required Unit
TemperatureDegrees Celsius (°C)
HumidityPercentage (%)
RainfallMillimeters (mm)
Wind SpeedMeters per second (m/s)
Wind DirectionDegrees (°)
Light IntensityLux (lx)
Canopy CoverPercentage (%)
Canopy HeightMeters (m)

3. Viewing & Downloading Data

  • Go to the Data page and log in with your Group ID and Group Password.
  • Image viewing: Click on photo thumbnails in the table to open full‑size field images.
  • Export: Use Download CSV to export the visible dataset for analysis and your final report.

4. Troubleshooting GPS, Uploads & Forms

  • GPS issues:
    • Ensure Location Services are enabled on your phone/tablet and for your browser.
  • Upload issues:
    • If the page is slow or “not responding” after submission, reduce the number of images or choose smaller files.
    • Check that your internet connection is stable before submitting.
  • Form navigation:
    • Use Previous and Next to review earlier steps without losing data.
    • If an error occurs, copy long text from the Notes field before refreshing the page.

5. Dashboard, Archive & Analysis

  • The EcoField Stats Dashboard summarises class data in real time based on your submissions.
  • It includes metrics such as Simpson’s Diversity Index calculated from your Species and Count inputs.
  • Use the dashboard to compare your group’s findings with the overall class and to support the data analysis section of your report.
  • The Archive link in the navigation bar may provide access to past cohorts’ datasets (if enabled by your instructor).
